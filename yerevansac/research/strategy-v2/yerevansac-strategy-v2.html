What it is
A bread boat. Bubbling cheese. A runny egg.

Adjaruli Khachapuri comes from the Adjara region of Georgia, on the coast of the Black Sea — where it's shaped like a small boat to honor the fishermen. Inside: salty sulguni and imeruli cheese, baked until it bubbles. A pat of butter and a fresh egg are stirred in tableside the moment it arrives.

You tear pieces of the crust, dip them in the molten center, and try not to make eye contact with the table next to you — because they're going to want one too.

How to eat one (without losing the broth)
Three steps. One delicious mistake to avoid.
1
Lift by the knot
The pleated top is your handle. Fork only. Never stab the body, ever — that's where the broth lives.
2
Bite the side, sip the broth
A small bite near the bottom. Slurp the hot, peppery broth before it spills.
3
Eat the rest, leave the knot
Tradition says you count the knots on your plate at the end. We won't judge if you eat them too.
